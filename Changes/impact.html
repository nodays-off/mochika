Impact from farm to cup

For us, coffee is personal. Every bean connects our family, our community in Nicaragua, and you.

We measure success in more than bags sold.

Fair relationships, not just transactions

  • Direct relationships with family farms and neighboring growers
  • Long-term buying commitments where possible
  • Transparent quality, pricing, and expectations

We start with our own family farms and a small circle of trusted neighbors, building the kind of relationships where everyone knows each other by name.

[Image: Handshake / conversation on farm]

A sustainability path we can stand behind

Now

Responsible farming practices and careful use of resources at origin, plus recyclable or low-waste options where possible.

Next

Moving toward more compostable and refill-friendly packaging as we grow and our volumes support it.

Long Term

Fully sustainable packaging across our line, and logistics choices that prioritize the health of our communities and environment.

[Image: Community education scene]

Rooted in community

Mochika is built around our home region in Nicaragua. As the brand grows, we plan to reinvest a portion of profits into education and community projects that support the families who make this coffee possible.

  • Education and skills development
  • Farm worker and family support
  • Community-led projects
